Introduction

The purpose of the Analytical Approach is to assist countries or regions build, modernize, or strengthen their national or regional biosafety and biosecurity systems to mitigate the risks of a natural, accidental or deliberate release of biological agents. A complete biosafety and biosecurity system encompasses a legal structure that provides a mandate for an oversight program, along with a policy framework, education and training, and a culture of responsible conduct.

The Analytical Approach:

  • Is a modular and scalable policy analysis tool with a biosafety and biosecurity context
  • Provides a structure for critical thinking that assists with defining country or region-specific issues and identifying feasible policy solutions to root causes
  • Supports risk management for moderate and high-risk pathogens and toxins using safety and security lenses
  • Includes practical guidance, an overview of common policy instruments, and options for combining them into a national program
  • Assists officials to identify and prepare sound recommendations to decision-makers or donors

Throughout, the Analytical Approach stresses the importance of outreach and engagement in an effort to get to know the various people and stakeholders that are implicated and including them in solutions, with a view to sustainability.

The objective of any policy is to create a change in behaviour among a target population, and the Analytical Approach provides a process to identify the desired behavioural change and the tools to bring it about.

Modules

This course contains 9 modules and a final quiz. Each module is designed to be flexible and modular to enable countries or regions to enter the methodology at different points depending on their current state.

Module 1: Identify the Current Situation

This module explains how to develop a picture of where, how, and who are using pathogens in your country or region, and what controls exist.

Module 2: Issue Identification, Risk Assessment, and Prioritization

This module explains how to go about the analysis of the situation and regulations in place – what works well, what does not work, who contributes to the issues, and where action needs to be taken.

Module 3: Scope and Objectives for New System Components

This module explains how to make choices on what risks to address, how much impact the national biosafety and biosecurity system will have, and by when.

Module 4: Policy Instruments

This module provides an exploration of policy instruments, their pros and cons.

Module 5: Combine Policy Instruments into National System Options

This module explains how to combine policy instruments into coherent national oversight system options.

Module 6: Implementation Approach

This module provides considerations on how your framework might be set up in your country/region and the role that senior decision-makers have in this process.

Module 7: Performance Measurement and Evaluation

This module explains how you can assess the impact of your final framework.

Module 8: Stakeholder Engagement

This module explains how you can consult with those who will be impacted by your framework to increase the chances that what you develop will be realistic, practical, and accepted.

Module 9: International Comparison

This module explains how to identify frameworks already in place that may work in your country/region (e.g., for ideas and to assess draft framework).
